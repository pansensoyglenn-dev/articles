Opinion: The Prague Arrest is a Smoke Screen for the High-Level Architects of Graft

An Opinion Piece

President Ferdinand “Bongbong” Marcos Jr. took to the podium yesterday to deliver what was framed as a major win for justice: the arrest of Zaldy Co. The former congressman and fugitive, notorious for his central role in the flood control fund mess, was apprehended in Prague for entering the Czech Republic without valid papers. The President promised a swift extradition, framing it as a triumph for the rule of law.

The stage is set, and the administration is ready for its standing ovation.

However, we should hold our applause. Accepting Zaldy Co as the "mastermind" of this heist is falling for a classic political diversion. It’s the age-old strategy of sacrificing a middleman to protect the bosses. While Co is a convenient scapegoat, the trail of diverted billions leads past his construction firm and straight to the gates of Malacañang and the office of former House Speaker Martin Romualdez.

Zaldy Co didn't build this machine; he just operated a lever. His company, Sunwest Construction, didn't just stumble upon billions in DPWH contracts—it dominated projects specifically within Romualdez’s home turf in Leyte. Whistleblowers and former aides have already connected the dots, alleging that the Speaker was the true force behind the curtain. Even Co, before he fled, hinted at a massive system of budget insertions involving hundreds of billions of pesos that required approval from the very top.

While Co sits in a foreign jail, the architects of this system remain insulated by power. President Marcos talks about justice, but in the Philippines, justice rarely has the stamina to climb the social ladder. The "wheels" of the law seem to lock up whenever the destination is the House of Representatives or the Presidential Palace.

This is exactly why corruption remains our national heartbeat. The consequences for high-level plunder are laughably light—interminable appeals, hospital arrests, or sentences that never actually result in prison time. When the "tax" on stealing billions is merely a temporary PR headache, there is no incentive for the elite to stop. Co may have been the one to get caught in the spotlight, but the infrastructure that enriched him is still being maintained by those currently in command.

Let’s call this arrest what it actually is: damage control. It’s a carefully timed photo-op to give the illusion of a crackdown. Meanwhile, the people who authorized the suspicious budget hikes and ignored the crumbling infrastructure that left Filipinos underwater are still enjoying the perks of their positions.

The Filipino people aren't just victims of the weather; they are being submerged by a tide of hypocrisy. We are told the "big fish" has been caught, but we forget that the truly massive predators are the ones who own the boat and the net.

A press release about an extradition isn't enough. The public deserves true accountability that doesn't stop at Zaldy Co. Unless the investigation probes the powerful figures who enabled and profited from this network, this arrest is nothing but theater—an expensive distraction while the real plunder continues unabated.
