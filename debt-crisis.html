Editorial: Inherited Ruin and the Looming Threat of Continuity

Philippine debt crisis, corruption legacy, and the 2028 election warning

The presidency of Rodrigo Duterte ended in 2022, but its grim financial and ethical footprint endures, shackling the Filipino people to decades of repayment. From 2016 to 2022, national debt exploded by ₱6.84 trillion, with foreign liabilities reaching ₱4.3 trillion—equivalent to over 60% of GDP by year's end. These loans, pitched as vital for "Build, Build, Build" infrastructure and COVID-19 relief, were instead riddled with systemic plunder.

Key Corruption Scandals Exposed

  • Pharmally Scandal: ₱11.27 billion funneled to a shell company for overpriced, substandard PPE—masks bought at ₱61 each when market price was ₱7.
  • COA Findings: ₱5.05 billion in unaccounted health emergency funds; ₱2.3 billion to "ghost" beneficiaries in social amelioration programs.
  • Total Diversion Estimate: Economists from Ateneo de Manila peg pandemic fund losses at ₱22-25 billion, per 2023 reports.
  • DPWH Anomalies: ₱67 billion in delayed or ghost infrastructure projects, as flagged by COA audits.

These aren't isolated incidents but symptoms of a governance model that weaponized emergency powers for enrichment. The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) warned in 2023 that debt servicing now consumes 40% of the national budget, up from 25% pre-Duterte.

The True Cost of Mismanagement

This debt tsunami isn't abstract—it's crushing Filipino families daily:

  • Inflation Surge: Food prices rose 12.3% in 2022 (PSA data), eroding purchasing power amid global shocks.
  • Tax Burdens: New excise taxes and VAT expansions added ₱150 billion in revenue demands, funneled straight to interest payments averaging ₱1 trillion annually.
  • Social Services Gutted: Public health spending per capita dropped 15% post-pandemic; DepEd faces ₱80 billion in classroom shortages.
  • Youth Impact: Unemployment for 15-24-year-olds hit 15.2% in 2023, as job-creating investments stalled.

Every peso siphoned via kickbacks denies a vaccine dose, a schoolbook, or a fisherman's subsidy. As economist Bem P. Tiu noted in a 2024 BusinessWorld op-ed: "We've mortgaged our children's future for elite windfalls."

The 2028 Danger: Dynasty of Secrecy

With 2028 looming, Sara Duterte's ascent risks entrenching this opacity. Her Office of the Vice President (OVP) alone spent ₱73.28 million in unverified "confidential funds" in 2022—disallowed by COA in 2024 for lacking receipts. Add DepEd's ₱125 million in similar irregularities under her watch, and the pattern echoes her father's playbook: authoritarian edicts, zero transparency, fiscal recklessness.

"To repeat the mistakes of the past is to condemn the nation to a lost decade where progress is sacrificed for a family’s political survival." — Adapted from former NEDA chief Arsenio Balisacan, 2023

Political analyst Richard Heydarian warns: "A Duterte restoration means doubled-down debt, weaponized impunity, and infrastructure theater over real development." Surveys show 68% of Filipinos prioritize anti-corruption (Pulse Asia, 2025), yet dynasty loyalists dominate narratives.

Breaking the Cycle: A Call to Action

Corruption's toll hits home—in empty sari-sari store shelves, overcrowded public hospitals, and OFW remittances stretched thin (now 10% of GDP). The Philippines' credit rating hovers at BBB-, per Fitch, signaling investor wariness.

Filipinos pay daily: ₱12/kg rice (up 50% since 2020), medicine shortages in 70% of rural health units, and youth migration rates at record highs.

At this crossroads, demand leaders who audit every peso, reject confidential slush funds, and invest in people over palaces. Reject the Dutertes' cycle of impunity. The 2028 ballot is your weapon—wield it to reclaim the republic from inherited ruin.
