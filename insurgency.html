URGENT CALL FOR JUSTICE: RELEASE ARTIST-ACTIVIST CEEKA GARZON

Kabataan Partylist – UST Artlets issues a vehement condemnation of the unlawful detention and brutal treatment of Charlize “Ceeka” Garzon, an artist-activist and alumna of De La Salle–College of Saint Benilde. Since her arrest on March 29 in San Jose, Occidental Mindoro, she has been subjected to physical and psychological torture while being held on fabricated charges.

State-Sponsored Repression and Fabricated Narratives

The Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) is actively attempting to frame Ceeka as an armed combatant—a move designed to legitimize the heavy military presence in Mindoro. To support this false narrative, state forces have disseminated manipulated photos of her with firearms to mislead the public.

Reported Violations Include:

  • Physical and Mental Torture: Inflicted by the 203rd Brigade, 68th Infantry Battalion, and the 2nd Civil-Military Operations unit.
  • Breach of International Law: A direct violation of the Comprehensive Agreement on Respect for Human Rights and International Humanitarian Law (CARHRIHL).
  • Denial of Due Process: Interrogation without legal representation and the restriction of communication with family and counsel.
  • Harassment of Supporters: Family members and paralegals were intimidated by a state-orchestrated "protest" and barred from seeing her for hours.

The Reality of Militarization

Ceeka is not a criminal; she is a youth leader who dedicated her art and energy to the marginalized communities of Mindoro. Her arrest is a symptom of the National Action Plan for Unity, Peace, and Development (NAP-UPD). While framed as a peace initiative, this "whole-of-nation" approach acts as a catalyst for:

  • Red-tagging and harassment of civilians.
  • Violence against farmers and Indigenous Peoples.
  • State-led repression of anyone challenging systemic injustice.

Our Demands

The Marcos Jr. administration and the AFP must be held accountable for the escalating human rights crisis in Mindoro and beyond. We demand an immediate end to the waste of public funds on propaganda and military aggression. These resources should instead be utilized for social services and addressing the core causes of poverty.

“The state targets individuals like Ceeka Garzon because they dare to demand genuine land reform and social justice. We will not be silenced.”
