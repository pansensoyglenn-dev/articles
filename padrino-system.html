The Padrino Perpetuum:
How Class Exploitation Fuels Endless Poverty

By: Glenn Junsay Pansensoy | Diplahan, Zamboanga Sibugay

In a nation of gleaming towers and sprawling slums, a paradox endures: a democracy on paper, but grinding poverty for over 20 million Filipinos. This isn't bad luck—it's the predictable output of a political machine designed for elite exploitation.

🏛️ 1. Dynasties: The Family Business of Power

The state serves the ruling class. In the Philippines, “political dynasties” dominate 80% of congressional seats.

  • The Padrino Logic: Power flows through personal ties, not merit.
  • The Feudal Graft: Families like the Marcoses, Dutertes, and Aquinos use infrastructure and relief goods to buy loyalty, treating public office like a family firm.
  • The Gap: While GDP grew at 6%, the top 1% hold 17% of national income. Corruption isn't a bug; it’s the system's logic.

🚩 2. The Youth Trap: Why Desperation Leads to Rebellion

The “Padrino” promise of trickle-down wealth has evaporated.

  • Unemployment: 49% youth unemployment in informal sectors (2025 SWS data).
  • The Breaking Point: Graduates face a job market dominated by cronies, with wages (₱20k) failing to meet living costs.
  • The Radical Alternative: This despair is fueling CPP-NPA recruitment. In places like Negros Occidental, where 70% of youth are landless, the state offers nothing, while revolutionary groups offer land and a sense of purpose.

✈️ 3. The “Export” of People

We rely on $37 billion in OFW remittances to mask the fact that we are exporting our best labor because the local economy is a “debt trap,” with 40% of the budget servicing foreign loans. Neoliberal policies privatize our resources (mining, telecom) while the profits flee abroad.

⚡ STRUCTURAL REALITY
While GDP grows, wealth concentrates. The Padrino system ensures every government project, license, and contract becomes a reward for loyalty, strangling genuine competition. Poverty is not accidental — it's the grease of patronage politics.

⚡ The Verdict: Reform or Rupture?

Bills to ban political dynasties fail because they ask the elite to vote against their own bank accounts.

True change requires more than “tweaks.” It requires a rupture of the capitalist property relations that keep the majority in chains. Until the Padrino Veil is torn, poverty will remain the price of elite rule—and our youth will continue to pay that price in blood.
